While Switzerland is not a member state of the European Union EU, it is strongly influenced by the European integration process. Since the referendum on the European Economic Area EEA in 1992, the relationship with the EU has been at the top of the political agenda, and Swiss citizens have been repeatedly asked to vote on EUrelated issues.1 Switzerlands European policy has remained a highly controversial issue all along, and it has strongly contributed to the redefinition of the cleavage structure and to changes in electoral competition and party system Kriesi et al. 2008.
