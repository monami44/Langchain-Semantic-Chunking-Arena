Adorno 2006, p. 4 famously drew an even stronger conclusion After Auschwitz, he argued, any appeal to the idea of progress would seem absurd given the scale of the catastrophe. For another thing, developmental views of history typically have a strong Eurocentric or Westerncentric bent. That is, they tend to present a historical story that positions European modernity as the outcome of a developmental learning process, and this story necessarily positions non or premodern Others as cognitively and normatively inferior to us Allen, 2016, p. 79.
