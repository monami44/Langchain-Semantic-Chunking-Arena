Moving embryos can be risky business for all parties, as was seen in a 2017 federal lawsuit when the embryos arrived at their destination in a nonviable state. The devastated intended parents sued for destruction of embryos resulting from improper shipping practices, including claims against the sending longterm storage facility party who packed the shipper, the shipping company employer of the worker who opened the container because he saw gas escaping, and the receiving fertility clinic party who unpacked the shipping container 40Lucio v.
