Where the change in the legal position of a person is caused by at least two distinct acts, the former providing the basis for the adoption of the latter binding act without having itself primary legal effect, then the Union courts may review the former act only indirectly, such as via Articles 267 and 277 TFEU. The judicial accountability mechanisms within the Single Supervisory Mechanism mirror the allocation of competences between the national authorities and the European Central Bank within the Single Supervisory Mechanism by providing for a separate but integrated system of judicial review.
